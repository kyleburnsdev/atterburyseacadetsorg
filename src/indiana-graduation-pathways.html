Putting Indiana Students on a Path to Success

Graduation Pathways Overview

The Indiana Department of Education embraces the principal that not all learning happens in a classroom and recognizes that education cannot be "one size fits all". Beginning with the graduating high school class of 2023, in addition to receiving their high school diploma Indiana students are required to achieve additional goals in order to graduate high school. This requirement and the supporting program is called Graduation Pathways.

Where Sea Cadets fits in

The Graduation Pathways program consists of three high level requirements:

  1. High School Diploma
  2. Learn and Demonstrate Employability Skills
  3. Postsecondary Ready Competencies
Participation in programs such as the United States Naval Sea Cadet Corps fits squarely in the Service-Based Learning Experience category to fulfill the Employability Skills requirement. Through participation in our program, students log a minimum of 160 contact hours per year with their local unit in addition to spending a minimum of five days per year at residential training programs focused on a variety of occupational focus areas. When it comes time to start preparations for graduation, we will provide a comprehensive training record which can be included as the student's "work product" to document their participation in the program.
